Velocity Component Ratio

Meaning:Shows the ratio of "preserve original" and "edit offset" components in predicted velocity at each step.
• Green (Preserve): Component along original velocity direction, higher means more preservation
• Orange (Edit): Component perpendicular to original velocity, higher means stronger editing

Angle to Original Velocity

Meaning:Angle between predicted velocity and original velocity (fixed direction from noise to original image).
• 0° = Completely preserve original direction, no editing
• 90° = Completely perpendicular to original, pure editing
• Smaller angle means more focus on preserving original, larger angle means more editing

Velocity Component Magnitude

Meaning:Shows absolute magnitude (L2 norm) of two velocity components at each step.
• Green (Preserve): Magnitude of component along original velocity direction
• Orange (Edit): Magnitude of perpendicular component
• Usually larger in early denoising stages, gradually decreases later

Distance to Original Image

Meaning:Current latent position and original latent Euclidean distance between them.
• Decreasing distance indicates trajectory approaching original image
• If distance decreases then increases, it means deviation from original starts midway (editing takes effect)
• Can observe when editing starts to significantly affect the trajectory
